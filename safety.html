Safety in Cape Town

Cape Town's safety reputation is often worse than the reality for tourists visiting the main areas. Use the same common sense you would in any major European city and you will have a wonderful, trouble-free experience.

Essential Safety Rules

  • 🚗

    Never leave valuables visible in your car

    Break-ins at parking areas and hiking trailheads are common. Always lock everything in the boot out of sight.

  • 🌙

    Never walk alone at night in unfamiliar areas

    Always take an Uber after dark. Share your location with someone you trust.

  • 🏧

    ATM safety

    Use ATMs inside shopping centres or bank branches only. Never accept help from strangers at ATMs. Consider a Wise or Revolut travel card.

  • 📱

    Phone and bag awareness

    Phone snatching happens in crowded areas. Keep your phone in your pocket at markets and beaches. Use a crossbody bag rather than a handbag.

  • 🌊

    Ocean swimming

    Only swim on beaches with lifeguards. Atlantic Ocean (Camps Bay, Clifton) has very strong currents and is cold. False Bay (Muizenberg, Fish Hoek) is warmer and calmer — better for families.

  • 🐒

    Baboon warning

    Near Cape Point and Simon's Town — never wind down your car windows or leave food visible. Baboons are bold and will break in.

INSIDER TIP Cape Town's safety reputation is far worse than the reality for tourists in the main visitor areas. Millions of visitors enjoy Cape Town every year completely safely. Don't let fear stop you — just be street-smart, as you would be in any major world city.

Payment & Banking Tips

  • 💳

    Cards vs Cash

    Most restaurants, hotels, shops and supermarkets accept cards. Always carry some cash (€10–€25) for tips, markets, smaller restaurants and hiking permit fees — many nature reserves are cash only.

  • 🏧

    ATM Safety

    Use ATMs inside shopping centres (V&A Waterfront, Canal Walk, Cavendish) or inside bank branches only. Never use street ATMs. Never accept help from strangers at ATMs — card skimming is a known risk.

  • 📲

    Travel Cards

    Wise or Revolut travel cards are highly recommended to avoid foreign transaction fees. Load Euros before you travel or draw local currency at ATM on arrival.

  • 🤝

    Tipping

    Tipping is standard and expected. Restaurants: 10–15% of bill. Petrol station attendants: €0.50–€1. Parking attendants: €0.50–€1. Hotel porters: €1–€3. Tour guides: €5–€10 per day.

  • 🛡️

    Travel Insurance

    Essential. Private medical clinics are excellent but expensive without cover. Netcare and Life Healthcare are the main private hospital networks. Ensure your policy covers adventure activities if you plan to hike, kite surf or mountain bike.

Practical Essentials

Best Time to Visit

November–March for summer — warm weather, outdoor activities, beach life, wine festivals and Cape Doctor winds perfect for kitesurfing. April–October for Winelands, Cederberg hiking, fewer crowds and lower prices.

Weather

Mediterranean climate. Summer (Nov–Mar) is hot and dry, 25–35°C. Winter (Jun–Aug) is cool and wet, 10–18°C. Weather changes rapidly on Table Mountain — always carry a windproof jacket even in summer.

Load Shedding

Download the EskomSePush app to check scheduled power cuts. Most hotels, restaurants and tourist attractions have generators — minimal impact on your trip, but good to know in advance.

SIM Card & Data

Buy a local SIM card at the airport on arrival. Vodacom and MTN are the most reliable networks with good 4G coverage. Data is very affordable — around €8 for 10GB is typical.

Tap Water

Cape Town tap water is completely safe to drink. After the severe 2018 drought, the city has recovered well. No need to buy bottled water.

Electricity

South Africa uses Type M plugs (large 3-pin round). Voltage is 230V. Bring a universal travel adaptor. USB charging is widely available.

Language

English is universally spoken in tourist areas, restaurants, hotels and shops. Afrikaans is the second most common language. A few words: "Lekker" = great. "Howzit" = how are you. "Braai" = BBQ.

Health

No malaria risk in Cape Town. No vaccinations specifically required but ensure routine vaccinations are up to date. Strong sunscreen is essential — UV levels are extremely high. Stay hydrated.

Driving Tips

Drive on the left. Speed limits strictly enforced by cameras. Zero tolerance for drink-driving. Four-way stops are common — first to arrive, first to go. Never leave anything visible in a parked car.
