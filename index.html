Cape Town's iconic attractions.

Icon

Table Mountain

Go early morning or late afternoon to avoid the heat and crowds. Always book the cable car online. Check the cloud cover first.

Robben Island

Where Nelson Mandela was imprisoned for 18 years. A deeply moving experience — book 2+ weeks ahead in peak season.

⏱ 3.5 hrs📍 Waterfront💳 R750

African penguins on a pristine beach. Genuinely magical. Combine with the full Peninsula drive for a perfect day out.

⏱ 1–2 hrs📍 Simon's Town💳 R220

The full Peninsula loop is one of the world's great road trips. Start at 8am, do Chapman's Peak both ways.

Cape Town's colourful Cape Malay quarter. Go early morning for photos before the crowds arrive. Incredible history.

Local knowledge

What the guidebooks don't tell you.

Honest tips from someone who actually lives here.

🛡️ Safety

Tourist areas are very safe. Use Uber exclusively — never unmarked taxis. Don't leave anything visible in a parked car. Take your old phone instead of your new one.

🏖️ Beaches

Skip Camps Bay (overpriced, crowded). Go to Llandudno instead — same mountain backdrop, a fraction of the people, dramatic granite boulders.

🍷 Wine

Durbanville Hills is the insider pick. Smaller estates, you can meet the winemaker, no tourist crowds. Diemersdal is the standout. Combine with MTB for the perfect day.

🥾 Hiking

Lion's Head before 7am on a weekday. The full moon hike is bucket-list. Don't hike Table Mountain on your first day — rest after the flight first.

🍽️ Food

Bree Street, not the Waterfront. Old Biscuit Mill on Saturdays. The Test Kitchen for a special occasion — book 6 weeks ahead. Kalk Bay for the freshest fish.

🚗 Getting Around

Rent a car from Day 2 onwards — it unlocks the Peninsula, wine estates and Cederberg. Chapman's Peak toll (R52) is worth every cent.
