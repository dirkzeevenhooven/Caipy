What the guidebooks miss.

Real local knowledge — not scraped from the internet.

Bo-Kaap
The colours aren't what you think.

Guidebooks say the houses were painted after apartheid. The real story goes deeper: when the Dutch colonial system forbade enslaved residents from owning property, all homes had to be painted white. The moment freed residents could finally buy their homes in the 19th century, they painted them every colour they could find. Freedom, made visible.

Beaches
Skip Camps Bay.

Go to Llandudno — same mountain backdrop, far fewer people, dramatic granite boulders. The beach most tourists never find.

Wine
Durbanville Hills, not Stellenbosch.

Smaller estates, no tourist crowds. Diemersdal is the standout — combine with mountain biking for the perfect day.

Hiking
Lion's Head before 7am.

On a weekday, before the crowds. The full moon hike is bucket-list. Never on your first day — rest after the flight.

Food
Bree Street, not the Waterfront.

Cape Town's best restaurants are on Bree Street — not at the Waterfront. The Test Kitchen for a special occasion (book 6 weeks ahead). Shortmarket Club for your first night. Kalk Bay for the freshest fish you'll ever eat.

Road Trip
The most beautiful scenic route.

Chapman's Peak Drive has wound along this cliff face since 1922 — nine kilometres of ancient granite plunging straight into the Atlantic. It's not just a road between Hout Bay and Noordhoek. Push on to Cape Point, loop back via Boulders Beach, and call it the best half-day of your trip.

Events
First Thursday. Every month.

On the first Thursday of every month, galleries, studios and restaurants on Bree Street throw open their doors after dark. Cape Town at its most electric — and it's free. See what's on →
