Every time a friend visits Cape Town, I send them the same message: "Ignore TripAdvisor. Here's what you actually want to do." This is that list — written down properly, with the context you need to actually enjoy these places the way locals do.

01  ·  Eat & Drink

01
Coffee · De Waterkant
Truth Coffee Roasting
Named the world's best coffee shop more than once. The steampunk interior alone is worth the visit — but the flat white is the reason you'll come back. Arrive before 9am or expect a queue.
Artivist Street, De Waterkant
02
Wine · Constantia
Beau Constantia
Perched high on the Constantia ridge with views that stretch to False Bay. The wine is excellent and the tapas menu is built for a long, slow afternoon. This is where I bring people who want to feel like they've discovered something.
Book ahead for weekends
03
Seafood · Kalk Bay
Kalky's
A fish-and-chips shack at the working harbour where actual fishermen sell their catch. Eat on the dock watching seals beg for scraps. No pretension, no menu surprises — just incredibly fresh fish at honest prices.
Cash only · lunch only
04
Sundowners · Green Point
The Duchess of Wisbeach
A neighbourhood bar that the Green Point locals treat as their living room. Low-key, unpretentious, great gin selection. The kind of place you wander into for one drink and stay for three hours.
No reservations needed
05
Brunch · Woodstock
The Old Biscuit Mill (Saturday Market)
Only open Saturday mornings, and only worth going to before 10am. By midday it's tourist chaos. Get there early for the genuine food market experience — local vendors, incredible variety, the real Cape Town creative crowd.
Saturdays 9am–2pm only

02  ·  Beaches & Water

06
Beach · Llandudno
Llandudno Beach
No parking lot. No vendors. No bar. That's exactly why it's perfect. You have to walk 10 minutes from wherever you park, which keeps the crowds out. The boulders, the cold Atlantic, the silence — this is what Cape Town looks like when nobody's performing for Instagram.
Best in late afternoon light
07
Tidal Pool · St James
St James Tidal Pool
The Atlantic Ocean at Cape Town temperatures (14°C on a warm day) is brutal. The St James tidal pool gives you sheltered, slightly warmer water with the same dramatic ocean backdrop. The brightly painted beach huts behind it are one of Cape Town's most photographed spots — for good reason.
Arrive by 9am in summer
08
Viewpoint · Signal Hill
Signal Hill Sunset
Every guidebook sends you to the top of Table Mountain for sunset. Locals drive to Signal Hill — you can drive almost to the summit, the view of the Atlantic horizon is unobstructed, and you're watching the same sun go down. Bring a bottle of wine. This is the move.
Drive up, park near the top

— Dirk Zeevenhooven, Cape Town local since 2014

03  ·  Neighbourhoods & Culture

09
Neighbourhood · Woodstock
Lower Woodstock on a Tuesday
Skip Bo-Kaap (beautiful but now mainly a photo queue). Woodstock is Cape Town's real creative neighbourhood — street art, independent studios, coffee shops that have been here for a decade, and a local energy that hasn't been packaged for tourists yet. Go on a weekday to see it properly.
Albert Road is the main drag
10
Market · Observatory
The Oranjezicht City Farm Market
Saturday morning at the V&A Waterfront but locally-run and genuinely good. Fresh produce, artisan food, local designers. The kind of market that actually reflects how Cape Townians live and eat — not a tourist performance.
Saturdays 8am–2pm, V&A Waterfront
11
Art · Cape Town CBD
Zeitz MOCAA (on a quiet morning)
The world's largest museum of contemporary African art. Most visitors go mid-morning on weekends and battle the crowds. Go on a Tuesday or Wednesday morning when it's nearly empty and the architecture — a converted grain silo — speaks for itself.
Weekday mornings are best
12
Drive · Atlantic Seaboard
Chapman's Peak at Golden Hour
Every visitor drives Chapman's Peak. Locals time it differently: northbound in late afternoon so the light hits the ocean as you drive back toward Cape Town. Stop at the viewpoint halfway for what might be the best view in the country.
Drive north (Hout Bay → Noordhoek direction) late afternoon

04  ·  Insider Knowledge

13
Timing · Table Mountain
The Cable Car Line Secret
The cable car queues can be brutal in peak season. Locals book the first car of the day (it leaves around 8am) or the last one down. You get the mountain to yourself at sunrise, or you catch the city lights at dusk. Both are infinitely better than the midday crush.
Book online the night before
14
Safety · Everywhere
The Phone Rule
Don't walk with your phone out in any area you're not completely certain about. This one habit eliminates 80% of the crime risk that affects tourists. Use your phone inside, in your car, or in established tourist areas. When in doubt, leave it in your bag.
Simple. Effective. Non-negotiable.
15
Local Habit · Citywide
The Noon Gun
Every day at exactly 12:00, a cannon fires from Signal Hill. You'll hear it across the entire city if you're anywhere central. Locals barely notice it anymore. Visitors always jump. It's one of those things that reminds you that Cape Town has been a city for a long time — since 1652 — and has its own rhythms.
Listen for it from anywhere in the City Bowl
