South Africa's
Finest Wines

The Cape Winelands produce some of the world's most exciting wines — at prices that would make a Burgundy producer weep. Dirk's guide to where to go, what to drink, and how to do it without a tour bus.

🍷
"Never do the winelands on a weekend in peak season. Never take a tour bus. Rent a car, go on a weekday, and call ahead — the winemaker will often pour your tasting personally."
The Cape wine guide

World-class wine, extraordinary scenery, and prices that make European wine lovers cry. Here's how to do it properly.

South Africa's wine industry is one of the world's best-kept secrets. Stellenbosch Cabernets compete with Bordeaux. Franschhoek Chenin Blancs rival the Loire. Hemel-en-Aarde Pinot Noirs are mentioned in the same breath as Burgundy. And you can taste all of it for a fraction of the price — if you know where to go.

01
💎 Dirk's Secret Pick
Hidden Gem Wine Region · 20 min from Cape Town

Durbanville Hills & Diemersdal

🚗 20 min from city
💰 R80–150 tasting
👤 No tourist buses
📅 Weekdays best
Dirk's Take
"The wine secret nobody tells you about. 20 minutes from the city. The winemaker at Diemersdal pours your tasting personally. Zero tourist buses. This is how wine should always be done."

While everyone floods to Stellenbosch, Durbanville Hills sits just 20 minutes north of the city, cool maritime breezes off the Atlantic producing exceptional Sauvignon Blanc and Pinotage. Diemersdal is the standout estate — family-owned for generations, the Louw family are often personally present for tastings.

Insider Tips
→ Call Diemersdal ahead — the winemaker Thys Louw sometimes does private barrel tastings
→ Try the MM Louw Sauvignon Blanc — world-class, R180 a bottle
→ Combine 3–4 Durbanville estates for a perfect full-day circuit
→ No Uber from the city — arrange a driver or use a designated driver

The right way

How to do the Winelands without a tour bus

1
Rent a car and go on a Tuesday or Wednesday — you'll have the estates almost to yourself
2
Choose one region per day — Stellenbosch, Franschhoek, and Constantia are each full days
3
Call ahead to book — most estates are happy to arrange private tastings for small groups
4
Designate a driver or arrange an Uber from Stellenbosch — don't drink and drive, ever
5
Buy wine to take home — Cape wine is extraordinary value and airlines allow 2 bottles in checked luggage
What to avoid

The wine tourist mistakes Dirk sees every weekend

✗
Going on a Saturday in December — you'll queue for tastings and share the road with tour buses
✗
Trying to do Stellenbosch AND Franschhoek in one day — they're an hour apart, you'll rush both
✗
Only going to the famous estates — the hidden ones like Diemersdal are always better value
✗
Eating at the Waterfront after wine — you're already in wine country, eat there instead
✗
Skipping Hemel-en-Aarde — it's the most exciting wine region in the country and most tourists never go
