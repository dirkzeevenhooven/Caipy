What makes a hidden gem

The places locals go when they want to escape the places tourists go

After 10+ years in Cape Town, these are the places Dirk still goes when he wants to feel like he has the city to himself. A secret beach between Clifton and Camps Bay. A wine estate where the winemaker pours your tasting personally. Kelp forests from a documentary that won an Oscar. These aren't tips from a blog — they're from someone who lives here.

💎 Most Secret
01
Hidden Gem · Bakoven, Atlantic Seaboard

Beta Beach & Bali Beach

⏱ 2–3 hrs 💰 Free 📍 Between Camps Bay & Clifton
Dirk's Take
"Secret coves between Camps Bay and Clifton. Almost completely private. You scramble down the rocks and suddenly you have your own beach. Bring beach shoes."

Park on Victoria Road in Bakoven and scramble down the rocks to find two hidden tidal pools and coves that almost no tourist ever discovers. Crystal clear water, dramatic boulders, and on a good day — complete solitude. This is what Clifton felt like 30 years ago.

How to find it
→ Park on Victoria Road, Bakoven — look for the rock scramble path down
→ Bring beach shoes — the rocks are uneven on the way down
→ Morning is best — goes into shade after 3pm
→ Do not post the exact location on Instagram — keep this one secret
