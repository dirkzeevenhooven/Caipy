Getting Around

🚗

You'll need a rental car for this itinerary

The Cape Peninsula drive and the Constantia wine estates are not accessible without your own wheels. An Uber works for city evenings, but Days 3 and 4 require a car.

Dirk's tip: Book at least 3 days in advance in peak season (Dec–Jan). An automatic SUV or hatchback is ideal for Cape Town's roads — comfortable on the mountain passes and easy to park in Sea Point.

1
Arrival Day — Settle In & Find Your Feet
Easy start · Sea Point · First sunset · No pressure
Morning / Arrival
Land, transfer to your accommodation, and do nothing. Unpack, shower, get oriented. Cape Town rewards the rested traveller. Don't try to cram your first afternoon — you have five days.
Afternoon
Walk the Sea Point Promenade — a 3km coastal path with mountain views, ocean pools and the real Cape Town in plain sight. Families, joggers, pensioners, beach volleyball. Watch the Atlantic crash against the rocks. End at the Sea Point Pavilion tidal pool for a swim if you're brave enough (water is cold).
Evening
Sunset drinks at Koi Bar or Café Caprice on the Camps Bay strip — watch the sun drop behind the Twelve Apostles with a glass of something cold. Then cross to Bree Street for dinner. Superette (if open), Wren, or Bocca. All brilliant, none will disappoint. Budget R250–400 per person.
💡
Insider Tip
Book Bree Street dinner reservations online before you land — good tables go fast, especially Thursday–Saturday.
🛡️
Safety & Timing
Uber from airport to accommodation. Uber back from Bree Street at night. Never walk to your car alone late evening in unfamiliar areas.
☁️
Plan B (Bad Weather)
Skip the promenade and head to the V&A Waterfront instead — good for a rainy first afternoon. The Two Oceans Aquarium is excellent.

2
Table Mountain & the City
Iconic views · Cape Malay culture · Kloof Street dinner
Morning
Book the Table Mountain cable car for 8:30am — early morning gives you the best light, fewer crowds, and the clearest views. Check the cloud cover the night before (tablecloth = car may not run). The summit walk takes 30–60 minutes at your own pace. 360° views of the Peninsula, both oceans, Robben Island.
Afternoon
Head to Bo-Kaap — the colourful Cape Malay neighbourhood with cobblestone streets, pastel houses and a 300-year history. Wander, photograph, grab a koeksister. Then walk 10 minutes to Signal Hill if you have energy — drive to the top for a second panoramic view of the city and harbour. Sunset from here is spectacular if you time it right.
Evening
Dinner on Kloof Street — Cape Town's coolest dining strip. Try The Black Sheep (book ahead, always full), La Mouette for refined dining, or Café du Cap for something lighter. Walk Kloof Street before choosing — the vibe changes every 50 metres.
💡
Insider Tip
Book the cable car online the night before — R380 return. If you miss the early slot, Signal Hill is a free alternative for views at sunset.
🛡️
Safety & Timing
Bo-Kaap is safe in the day — go before 10am for photos without crowds. Don't photograph locals without permission. Uber between areas after dark.
☁️
Plan B (Bad Weather)
If the mountain is clouded out, do Robben Island instead (book in advance) — a deeply moving 3.5-hour experience whatever the weather.

3
Cape Peninsula Full Day
Chapman's Peak · Boulders Beach Penguins · Cape Point · Noordhoek sunset
Morning — Early Start
Leave by 7:30am. Drive Chapman's Peak — one of the most dramatic coastal roads on earth. Stop at every viewpoint. It takes 20 minutes if you don't stop; allow 1 hour if you do it properly. Head to Boulders Beach — 3,000 African penguins that walk up to you. One of the most extraordinary wildlife encounters in the world. R220 entry.
Afternoon
Drive to Cape of Good Hope — the dramatic southwestern tip of Africa. The Flying Dutchman funicular saves the climb. Walk the trails, feel the wind, stand at the southwesternmost point in Africa. The fynbos is extraordinary. Stop at Simon's Town on the way back for a late lunch at Seaforth Restaurant — tables right on the water.
Evening
Watch the sunset from Noordhoek Beach — 8km of wild, deserted beach with the Chapman's Peak cliffs turning pink. Stop at the Noordhoek Farm Village for dinner: The Foodbarn is exceptional (book ahead). Drive back the coastal route via Hout Bay.
💡
Insider Tip
Baboons at Cape Point are wild and bold — lock your car, never feed them, keep windows closed. They will open a door if you leave it unlocked.
🛡️
Safety & Timing
This is a full 10–11 hour day. You need a hire car. The roads are safe and well-maintained. Don't drive Chapman's Peak in heavy rain — it closes.
☁️
Plan B (Bad Weather)
Do Boulders Beach only (penguins don't mind the rain) and swap the Point for the Kalk Bay harbour, galleries and coffee on the main road.

4
Constantia Wine Route & Kirstenbosch
World-class wine · Botanical gardens · Relaxed dinner
Morning
Drive to Constantia — the oldest wine-producing area in the Southern Hemisphere, 20 minutes from the city centre. Start at Groot Constantia (South Africa's oldest estate, 1685), then Klein Constantia for their famous Vin de Constance dessert wine. Allow 1.5 hours per estate. Tastings from R100–200 per person.
Afternoon
Kirstenbosch National Botanical Garden — one of the great botanical gardens of the world, built into the eastern slopes of Table Mountain. Walk the treetop canopy walkway, explore the Cape Fynbos section, have a lazy picnic on the lawn. Allow 2–3 hours. Entry R220. Summer: check for Sunday concerts — legendary.
Evening
Relaxed dinner back in Cape Town. If you want something special: The Test Kitchen (book months ahead) or FYN near the Company's Garden. If you want easy: return to Bree Street and try somewhere you missed on Night 1. Or La Colombe in Constantia itself — one of Africa's best restaurants.
💡
Insider Tip
Designate a driver or use Uber between estates — the Constantia estates are 5–10 minutes apart. A wine tour driver costs R600–900 for the day and is worth every rand.
🛡️
Safety & Timing
Constantia is one of Cape Town's safest and most affluent suburbs. Kirstenbosch closes at 6pm in winter, 7pm in summer. Check hours before going.
☁️
Plan B (Bad Weather)
Wine estates are perfect in any weather — skip Kirstenbosch and add a third estate. Buitenverwachting has an exceptional restaurant for a long rainy lunch.

5
Waterfront, Culture & One Last Hidden Gem
Zeitz MOCAA · V&A Waterfront · Send-off dinner
Morning
Walk the V&A Waterfront — the working harbour with Table Mountain as backdrop. This is one of the great urban views on earth. Don't eat here (overpriced) but walk, sit, watch the boats. Visit the Zeitz Museum of Contemporary African Art — the world's largest museum of contemporary African art, inside a converted grain silo. Allow 2 hours. Extraordinary building alone.
Afternoon
Depending on energy: explore De Waterkant (Cape Town's design district), browse Neighbourgoods Market (Saturday only at Old Biscuit Mill), or return to any spot you want to see one more time. If you haven't done it yet — Lion's Head hike takes 2 hours and gives arguably the best view in Cape Town. Check safety: go with others or a guide.
Evening
Final dinner at a place off the tourist trail. The Pot Luck Club in Woodstock (small plates, rooftop, book ahead), Harbour House in Kalk Bay (seafood, right on the harbour), or ask your accommodation for their honest local recommendation — that's always the best option on your last night.
💡
Insider Tip
Zeitz MOCAA is closed on Tuesdays. Book tickets online. The Silo Hotel rooftop bar is above it — incredible sundowner spot even if you're not staying.
🛡️
Safety & Timing
The Waterfront is safe at all hours. Woodstock (Biscuit Mill) is fine in the day, use Uber at night. Don't walk from Woodstock to the city centre at night.
☁️
Plan B (Bad Weather)
A full rainy day at Zeitz MOCAA plus lunch and shopping at the V&A Waterfront is genuinely enjoyable. The Two Oceans Aquarium is also excellent in rain.
