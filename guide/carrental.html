Renting a Car in
Cape Town

A car is non-negotiable if you want to explore beyond the city. The Peninsula drive, the Winelands, Chapman's Peak — none of it works without wheels. Uber is perfectly fine for getting around town, but rent a car for at least part of your trip. You won't regret it.

Do you actually need one?
It depends entirely on how you're spending your days
No car needed
City-only travel

If your trip is Sea Point, the Waterfront, Bo-Kaap, and the City Bowl, Uber works brilliantly and is cheap by international standards. Parking in the city is a pain anyway. Save the car rental for when you actually need it.

Car essential
Peninsula & beaches

Llandudno, Boulders Beach, Cape Point, Chapman's Peak — there is simply no other way to do this properly. No Uber driver will wait at Cape Point for two hours. This is the single biggest reason to rent a car in Cape Town.

Car or tour group
Winelands day trip

You need a car to get to Stellenbosch or Franschhoek — but you can't drink and drive. Either assign a non-drinking driver, or book a dedicated wine tour. Plenty of excellent half-day and full-day options run from Cape Town.

Car essential
Hermanus & day trips

Hermanus for whale watching, the Cederberg for landscapes, Langebaan for the lagoon — these are all car trips. None are reachable by public transport in any practical way. Budget a few extra days and go explore.

Driving tips
Things worth knowing before you get behind the wheel
  • 🇬🇧
    Drive on the left South Africa follows UK road rules — left-hand traffic, overtake on the right. It catches first-timers off guard at roundabouts. Take the first day slowly and you'll adapt faster than you think.
  • 🗺️
    Stick to main routes Avoid driving through townships — not because it's always dangerous, but because navigating without local knowledge creates unnecessary situations. N1, N2, M3, and the Peninsula routes are all completely fine.
  • 🏔️
    Chapman's Peak toll: ~R55. Worth every cent. One of the most spectacular coastal drives in the world. The toll is a bargain. Go late afternoon with the light coming in from the west — you'll understand why people move to Cape Town.
  • 🅿️
    Use guarded parking only In the city especially — look for official parking garages or lots with an attendant. Many areas have informal car guards (tip R5–10 when you return). Never leave your car on an unlit street overnight.
  • 🚫
    Nothing visible in the car Ever. Bags on seats, cameras on dashboards, sunglasses in the cupholder — all of it out of sight, in the boot or with you. This applies across South Africa, not just Cape Town.
  • 📱
    Google Maps works perfectly Download the Cape Town area for offline use before you leave your accommodation. Data can be patchy on the Peninsula and in the Winelands. Offline maps have saved me more than once.
  • ⛽
    Petrol stations: Engen and BP are reliable Attendants fill your tank for you — that's just how it works here. Tip R5–10. Pay by card wherever possible. Don't let the tank get below a quarter on the Peninsula — stations are further apart than they look.
  • 📸
    Speed cameras on the N1 and N2 Common and unforgiving. Speed limits are taken seriously, and fines follow you internationally through the rental company. Set your speed alerts and stay 10km/h below the limit on highways.

On insurance: CDW (Collision Damage Waiver) is usually included in the base rate — but always check the excess amount. Standard excess can be R10,000–25,000, meaning that's what you pay out of pocket if anything happens before insurance kicks in. You can reduce this to near zero for an extra R100–200/day. Worth considering if you're doing mountain or coastal drives.
