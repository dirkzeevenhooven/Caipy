The hiking guide

Cape Town is one of the world's great hiking cities. Most people only do Table Mountain. Here's what they're missing.

Within 30 minutes of the city centre you can be on a mountain summit with 360° views of two oceans. The key is timing — go early, before the heat and the crowds. Dirk hikes before 7am most mornings. These six routes are the ones he comes back to again and again.

01
🥾 Dirk's Favourite
Iconic Hike · Signal Hill Road

Lion's Head

⏱ 2 hrs circular
📏 5km loop
⬆️ 669m summit
💰 Free
🅿️ Signal Hill Rd
Difficulty
Moderate
Dirk's Take
"Before 7am on a weekday you'll almost have it to yourself. The full moon hike is bucket-list. I've done it dozens of times and it never gets old."

Cape Town's most iconic hike — a circular trail around and over a distinctive 669m peak that sits between Table Mountain and the Atlantic. The 360° views take in the city, Camps Bay, the Twelve Apostles, and on clear days, the Cape Peninsula stretching to the horizon. Chains and ladders help on the steeper summit sections.

Insider Tips
→ Start before 7am on weekdays — almost to yourself, perfect light for photos
→ Full moon hike is free — check dates at SA Astronomical Observatory, book parking early
→ Chains and ladders on the summit — not suitable for severe vertigo
→ Signal Hill Road parking — arrive by 6:30am to guarantee a spot
→ Go clockwise — easier approach, more dramatic summit arrival

Bucket List Experience

The Full Moon Hike
on Lion's Head

Once a month, hundreds of Cape Town locals hike Lion's Head by moonlight. No torches needed — the full moon lights the entire mountain. The views of the city at night from the summit are extraordinary. This is one of the great free experiences anywhere on earth.

→ Free — no booking required, just show up at Signal Hill Road parking
→ Start at sunset — reach the summit as the moon rises
→ Bring a headtorch as backup and wear warm layers — it gets cold at the top
→ Check full moon dates: next ones are always listed on the City of Cape Town website
What to bring on every hike
💧
Water
Minimum 1.5L per person. Table Mountain dehydrates fast in summer. There is no water on the mountain.
🧴
Sunscreen
SPF 50 minimum. The UV index in Cape Town summer regularly hits 10+. You will burn faster than you think.
👟
Proper Shoes
Trail shoes or hiking boots for Lion's Head and Table Mountain. Flip flops have caused serious accidents on the mountain.
🧥
Wind Layer
Even in summer, the summit can be 10–15°C colder than the city. A light windproof jacket is essential.
⚠️
Mountain Safety — Non-Negotiable
Table Mountain claims lives every year from unprepared hikers. Never hike alone. Always tell someone your route and expected return time. Check the weather forecast — the mountain creates its own weather and conditions change fast. If you see the tablecloth forming, descend immediately. Emergency: Cape Town Mountain Rescue — 021 937 0300.

Outdoors · Cape Town
Mountain Biking

From beginner-friendly forest trails to technical mountain singletrack, Cape Town has some of the best mountain biking in Africa. Tokai Forest is the perfect starting point; Jonkershoek will test experienced riders.

Best Trails
Tokai Forest & Constantia Greenbelt

Tokai's pine forest trails are well-maintained, clearly marked, and ideal for all levels. Connect into the Constantia Greenbelt for longer rides with ocean views. Parking at Tokai Arboretum.

Find on Maps →
Advanced · Stellenbosch
Jonkershoek Valley

World-class singletrack through a mountain nature reserve 45 minutes from Cape Town. Technical climbs, fast descents, and spectacular scenery. The Swellendam Trail is a bucket-list ride.

jonkershoek.co.za →
Rentals & Guided Rides
Downhill Adventures

Cape Town's most reputable MTB operator. Guided rides from Table Mountain, bike rentals, and a range of routes for all levels. Based at the lower cable car station.

downhilladventures.com →
Dirk's Tip
"Tokai on a weekday morning is one of Cape Town's great free pleasures — pine forest, mountain air, and almost nobody around. Jonkershoek is worth the drive to Stellenbosch if you're an experienced rider. Take the Swellendam trail and reward yourself with a wine tasting after."
📅 When to go: Year-round · best Oct–Apr
⏱ Duration: 1–4 hours
💰 Cost: Free (own bike) · from R350 rental

Outdoors · Cape Town
Surfing, Kite & Wind

Cape Town has world-class conditions for surfing, kitesurfing, windsurfing and wing foiling. Muizenberg is the best beginner surf beach in South Africa. Big Bay is the kitesurfing capital of Africa.

Beginner Surfing · False Bay
Muizenberg Beach

Warm water (by Cape Town standards), long gentle rollers, and a thriving surf school scene. Gary's Surf School has been teaching here for decades. The colourful beach huts are iconic. Best for total beginners and intermediate surfers.

garysurf.com →
Kite & Wind · Atlantic
Big Bay & Bloubergstrand

Africa's kitesurfing capital. The Cape Doctor (SE trade wind) delivers consistent 25–35 knot sessions from October to March. Cape Sport Centre offers lessons, gear rental and storage for kitesurfers, windsurfers and wing foilers.

capesportcentre.co.za →
Advanced Surfing
Llandudno & Dungeons

Llandudno is a powerful, beautiful beach break for experienced surfers. Dungeons — off Hout Bay — is one of the world's great big wave spots, active in winter swells. Not for the faint-hearted.

Find on Maps →
Dirk's Tip
"Muizenberg is where every Cape Town surfer learns — warm water, forgiving waves, and a great vibe. If you're into kitesurfing or wing foiling, Big Bay in October–March is world-class. The Cape Doctor blows like clockwork and the Table Mountain backdrop makes it surreal."
📅 Surfing: Year-round
📅 Kite/Wind: Oct–Mar (SE winds)
💰 Lessons from: R450 per session

Outdoors · Cape Town
Rock Climbing & Abseiling

Table Mountain is one of the world's great climbing destinations — hundreds of routes from beginner to expert. The 112-metre abseil off the top is one of the most dramatic experiences in Africa.

Iconic · Table Mountain
Table Mountain Abseil

A 112-metre controlled abseil off the top of Table Mountain — one of the highest commercial abseil sites in the world. Run by Abseil Africa, who have been operating for over 25 years. No experience needed. Pure nerve.

abseilafrica.co.za →
Bouldering · World-Class
Rocklands & Paarl Rock

Rocklands in the Cederberg (3hrs north) is a world-renowned bouldering destination. Paarl Rock is 45 minutes away — enormous granite domes with routes for all levels. City Rock in Cape Town offers indoor climbing year-round.

cityrock.co.za →
Guided Climbing
Table Mountain Routes

Dozens of traditional and sport climbing routes on the mountain itself — India Venster, Nursery Buttress and Platteklip Gorge area. Cape Town Rock Guides offer half-day and full-day guided climbing for all levels.

capetownrock.co.za →
Dirk's Tip
"Do the Table Mountain abseil. It looks terrifying from the top — and it is — but you'll never forget it. Abseil Africa has an impeccable safety record over 25+ years. Book for sunrise if you can. The light on the City Bowl below is extraordinary."
📅 When to go: Year-round (avoid wind days)
💰 Abseil: from R1,100 per person
⏱ Abseil duration: ~2.5 hours total

Outdoors · Cape Town
Trail Running

Cape Town is a global trail running destination. The mountain is criss-crossed with world-class routes — from a 5km Lion's Head loop to multi-day epics along the Hoerikwaggo Trail.

Best Route · 5km
Lion's Head Loop

5km, 400m elevation, spectacular views of the Atlantic and City Bowl. Run it at sunrise and you'll have the chains and ladders to yourself. One of the best short trail runs anywhere in the world.

Find on Maps →
Epic Route
Two Oceans & Constantia Trails

The Two Oceans Ultra (56km, run every Easter) follows the Cape Peninsula — one of the world's most beautiful ultra routes. The Constantia forest trails are ideal for regular training runs: shaded, well-marked, and beautiful.

twooceansmarathon.org.za →
Free · Every Saturday
Parkrun Cape Town

Multiple Parkrun events across Cape Town every Saturday at 8am — free, timed, community-run 5km events. Green Point, Rondebosch, and Constantia are the most scenic. Just register once at parkrun.co.za.

parkrun.co.za →
Dirk's Tip
"The Lion's Head loop at sunrise is a trail runner's dream — 5km, chains and ladders, 360° Atlantic views. Be there by 6:30am in summer to beat the heat and the crowds. Wear trail shoes, take water, and don't rush the chains section."
📅 When to go: Year-round · best May–Sep (cooler)
💰 Cost: Free (self-guided)
⏱ Lion's Head loop: 1.5–2.5 hours

Outdoors · Cape Town
Sea Kayaking & SUP

Paddle with African penguins at Simon's Town, explore the sea caves of Llandudno, or SUP across the glassy waters of False Bay at sunrise. Cape Town's coastline is extraordinary from the water.

Must-Do · Simon's Town
Kayaking with Penguins

Launch from Simon's Town and paddle alongside African penguins in their natural habitat — one of Cape Town's most magical experiences. Real Cape Adventures run guided sunrise and morning sessions with expert guides.

realcapeadventures.com →
SUP & Flat Water
Milnerton Lagoon & False Bay

Milnerton Lagoon is Cape Town's best flat-water SUP spot — sheltered, calm, and with Table Mountain as the backdrop. False Bay sunrise SUP sessions from Simonstown are unforgettable on a still morning.

Find on Maps →
Sea Caves · Atlantic
Llandudno Sea Cave Kayak

Guided sea kayaking tours through the dramatic granite sea caves and kelp forests along the Atlantic Seaboard. Ocean Experiences run small-group guided tours from Camps Bay and Llandudno. Calm day essential.

oceanexperiences.co.za →
Dirk's Tip
"The sunrise kayak with penguins from Simon's Town is one of Cape Town's best-kept secrets. You're on the water before the tourist boats arrive, paddling alongside penguins with the Hottentots Holland mountains turning pink behind you. Book at least 2 days ahead."
📅 When to go: Year-round · calm mornings best
💰 Guided tours from: R650 per person
⏱ Duration: 2–3 hours

Outdoors · Cape Town
Diving & Snorkelling

Cold water (12–18°C), extraordinary kelp forests, and rich marine life make Cape Town a world-class diving destination. A wetsuit is non-negotiable — the reward is extraordinary.

Best Dive Sites
Boulders Beach & Miller's Point

Boulders Beach offers snorkelling with African penguins in the shallows — extraordinary and completely unique. Miller's Point in Simon's Town has excellent shore diving with diverse marine life, caves, and kelp forests.

Find on Maps →
Kelp Forests · Atlantic
Camps Bay & Oudekraal

The kelp forests off Oudekraal (between Camps Bay and Hout Bay) are among the most biodiverse dive sites in South Africa — catsharks, octopus, klipfish, and rays. Orca Industries operate charters from here.

orcaindustries.co.za →
Dive School & Courses
Pisces Divers

One of Cape Town's most established dive schools — PADI open water courses, advanced diver training, guided dives, and equipment rental. Based in Simon's Town, ideally positioned for False Bay diving.

piscesdivers.co.za →
Dirk's Tip
"The water is cold — don't let anyone tell you otherwise. A 5mm wetsuit minimum, 7mm if you run cold. The reward is extraordinary: kelp forests that feel like cathedrals, penguins underwater, and marine life that most divers never see. Oudekraal on a calm day is otherworldly."
📅 When to go: Year-round · Feb–May best visibility
🌡 Water temp: 12–18°C (wetsuit essential)
💰 Guided dives from: R550 per person
🦈
Day Trip · Gansbaai
Shark Cage Diving

Two hours from Cape Town, Gansbaai is the great white shark capital of the world. Shark Alley — the narrow channel between Geyser Rock and Dyer Island — is one of the ocean's most dramatic environments.

World's Best · Gansbaai
Shark Alley

The channel between Geyser Rock (a Cape fur seal colony of 50,000+) and Dyer Island attracts great white sharks year-round. No diving experience needed — the cage is surface-level. You just need to be willing.

Find on Maps →
Top Operator
Marine Dynamics

Gansbaai's most respected shark diving operator — conservation-focused, small groups, and genuinely committed to shark welfare. Their onboard marine biologists make the experience educational as well as extraordinary.

marinedynamics.co.za →
Alternative
White Shark Projects

Research-based shark diving with a strong conservation focus. Full-day trips from Cape Town including transfer, breakfast, and a full briefing. One of the most reputable operators in Gansbaai for 20+ years.

whitesharkprojects.co.za →
Dirk's Tip
"Book the earliest boat — sea conditions are calmer in the morning and shark activity is higher. Take seasickness tablets the night before if you're prone. This is genuinely life-changing. Face-to-face with a 4-metre great white through a cage is something you'll be talking about for the rest of your life."
📅 Best season: Year-round · May–Sep peak activity
🚗 Distance: ~2 hours from Cape Town
💰 Full day from: R2,200 per person
🐋
Seasonal · Jun–Nov
Whale Watching

Every year southern right whales migrate from Antarctica to the Cape to calve in the sheltered bays. Hermanus is the whale-watching capital of the world. From June to November, this is one of nature's greatest shows.

Land-Based · World-Class
Hermanus Cliff Path

Walk the 12km cliff path above Walker Bay and watch southern right whales breach, spy-hop and nurse their calves below. September is peak season. No boat needed — the whales come within metres of the shore.

Find on Maps →
Boat Tours · False Bay
Simon's Town Boat Company

Whale-watching boat tours from Simon's Town into False Bay — southern rights and humpbacks are regularly spotted. Small group sizes, experienced guides, and excellent wildlife viewing from October to December.

boatcompany.co.za →
Wine + Whales
Hermanus + Creation Wines

The best Cape Town day trip combination: whale watching from the Hermanus cliffs in the morning, then a wine tasting at Creation Wines above Walker Bay in the afternoon. Creation's Hemel-en-Aarde location overlooks the whale nursery.

creationwines.com →
Dirk's Tip
"Go to Hermanus in September. The southern rights are at peak numbers, the weather is still cool and clear, and the cliff path is one of the world's great wildlife walks. Combine it with a tasting at Creation Wines overlooking Walker Bay — whales breaching in the bay while you sip Hemel-en-Aarde Pinot Noir is Cape Town at its finest."
📅 Season: June–November
🚗 Hermanus: 1h 20min from Cape Town
💰 Cliff path: Free · Boat tours from R850

Outdoors · Cape Town
Paragliding

Signal Hill is one of the world's most spectacular tandem paragliding launch sites — Atlantic Ocean views, the City Bowl below, Table Mountain at eye level. No experience needed for a tandem flight.

Tandem Flights · Signal Hill
Birdmen Paragliding

Cape Town's most established paragliding operator. Tandem flights launch from Signal Hill and land on the Sea Point beachfront — a 15-minute flight over one of the world's great urban panoramas. Professional pilots, small groups.

birdmen.co.za →
Alternative Operator
Parapax

Another reputable tandem paragliding operator flying Signal Hill. Flexible booking, weather-dependent scheduling, and pilots with thousands of hours. Packages include transfer to the launch site from Sea Point.

parapax.com →
Learn to Fly
Paragliding Courses

Cape Town's consistent winds and varied terrain make it an ideal place to learn paragliding. Both operators offer beginner courses over 5–7 days. By the end you'll be flying solo on Signal Hill.

birdmen.co.za →
Dirk's Tip
"The Signal Hill tandem flight is one of those experiences that completely reframes the city. You're floating at Table Mountain's eye level, the Atlantic stretching to the horizon, the City Bowl spread below you. Book for the late afternoon on a clear day — the light is extraordinary."
📅 When to go: Year-round · SE wind days (summer)
⏱ Flight duration: 10–20 minutes
💰 Tandem from: R1,200 per person

Outdoors · Cape Town
Skydiving

Jump over Stellenbosch with Table Mountain, the Cape Peninsula and the Atlantic Ocean all visible simultaneously. The freefall view is unlike anything else on earth.

Tandem Skydiving
Skydive Cape Town

Tandem skydives from 10,000ft over the Stellenbosch wine valley. Table Mountain, the Cape Peninsula and two oceans visible during freefall. No experience needed — you're attached to an instructor throughout. GoPro video packages available.

skydivecapetown.com →
What to Expect
The Full Experience

Training briefing (30 min), kit-up, flight to altitude (20 min), 30–45 seconds of freefall at 200km/h, then 5 minutes of parachute flight over the vineyards. Total experience is around 3 hours. Minimum age 18, max weight 100kg.

Book →
Dirk's Tip
"The freefall view is genuinely one of the best on earth — Table Mountain, the Cape Peninsula, Stellenbosch vineyards, and both oceans simultaneously. Book for a weekday morning to beat the queues and get the calmest wind conditions. Don't skip the video package — you'll want the evidence."
📅 When to go: Year-round · summer clearest
💰 Tandem from: R2,100 per person
📍 Location: Stellenbosch airfield

Outdoors · Cape Town
Golf

Cape Town has world-class golf — from courses with Table Mountain as a backdrop to Winelands fairways set among Franschhoek vineyards. Green fees are exceptional value by international standards.

World-Class · Paarl
Pearl Valley Golf Estate

Jack Nicklaus-designed course in the Franschhoek Valley — one of the most beautiful golf courses in Africa. Mountain backdrop, immaculate fairways, and a clubhouse with outstanding views. Worth the drive from Cape Town.

pearlvalley.co.za →
Winery Setting · Stellenbosch
Steenberg Golf Club

18 holes at a 300-year-old wine estate in Constantia Valley — mountain views, mature trees, and wine tasting at the estate after your round. One of the most scenic courses in the Western Cape.

steenberghotel.com →
City Course
Metropolitan Golf Club

The Metropolitan in Sea Point is Cape Town's most central golf course — 18 holes walking distance from the city centre, with Table Mountain and Signal Hill as the backdrop. Great value for a city course.

metropolitangc.co.za →
Dirk's Tip
"Pearl Valley is worth the trip to Paarl — one of the most beautiful courses in Africa, Jack Nicklaus-designed, and exceptional value compared to European or American green fees. Combine it with a morning at the Franschhoek market and wine tasting at Babylonstoren for a perfect Saturday."
📅 When to go: Year-round · early morning best
💰 Green fees from: R450 (city) to R2,500 (Pearl Valley)
🚗 Pearl Valley: 1hr from Cape Town

Outdoors · Cape Town
Horse Riding

Ride along Noordhoek's 8km beach with the Sentinel mountains as a backdrop, or canter through the fynbos above Muizenberg. Cape Town's beach rides are among the best in the world.

Best Beach Ride · Noordhoek
Sleepy Hollow Horse Riding

Ride along Long Beach, Noordhoek — one of the longest and most dramatic beaches on the Cape Peninsula. Sleepy Hollow has been operating here for decades, with horses suited to all experience levels. Sunset rides are magical.

sleepyhollowhorseriding.co.za →
Alternative · Southern Suburbs
Imhoff Farm

Farm rides through fynbos and along Kommetjie beach from the historic Imhoff Farm village. Great for families and beginners. The farm also has a market, restaurants and a snake park for a full day out.

imhofffarm.co.za →
Dirk's Tip
"Noordhoek on horseback at sunset is something I recommend to everyone. The beach is 8km long, almost deserted, and the mountains behind it are extraordinary. Book the 2-hour sunset ride with Sleepy Hollow and take it slow — this isn't a rush."
📅 When to go: Year-round · sunset rides Oct–Mar
💰 Rides from: R500 per person (1 hour)
📍 Location: Noordhoek Valley

Outdoors · Cape Town
Fishing

Cape Town offers world-class fishing — yellowfin tuna off Cape Point, snoek in the winter, and shore angling along some of the most dramatic coastline on earth.

Deep Sea · Cape Point
Yellowfin Tuna Charters

January to May, yellowfin tuna run off Cape Point in numbers. Deep sea charters from Hout Bay harbour — full-day trips targeting yellowfin, yellowtail, and dorado. The fish are genuine trophy quality and the scenery is extraordinary.

Hout Bay Harbour →
Winter Season
Snoek Fishing

Snoek is Cape Town's most iconic local fish — fierce fighters and extraordinary on the braai. The season runs June–August off Hout Bay and Cape Point. Day charters are affordable and genuinely fun — even if you've never fished before.

Hout Bay Harbour →
Shore Fishing
Miller's Point & Scarborough

Shore angling at Miller's Point in Simon's Town is accessible, scenic, and productive year-round. Scarborough on the Atlantic side offers excellent rock angling with dramatic Cape Point scenery. No charter needed — just a rod and a tide table.

Find on Maps →
Dirk's Tip
"A yellowfin tuna caught off Cape Point, straight onto the braai the same evening with a cold beer and Table Mountain in the background — this is Cape Town living at its absolute best. Book a January–March charter from Hout Bay. It's worth every rand."
📅 Tuna season: Jan–May
📅 Snoek season: Jun–Aug
💰 Charters from: R1,200 per person (shared boat)
