Cape Town
Markets

Cape Town's markets are a world of their own — artisan food, design, craft, wine, and live music. These are the ones worth your Saturday morning.

Woodstock · Must-visit
Old Biscuit Mill / Neighbourgoods Market
Every Saturday · 09:00–14:00

The original and the best. The Old Biscuit Mill in Woodstock is Cape Town's most celebrated creative hub — and on Saturdays it transforms into the Neighbourgoods Market, where the city's food scene comes to show off. Sourdough, oysters, craft beer, fresh pasta, local cheese — all under one roof. Gets busy by 10am. Go early, stay late.

Website →
Dirk's Tip

Arrive by 9:30am to avoid queues at the popular stalls. The duck confit croissant and the fresh oysters are non-negotiable. Grab coffee from Truth first — it's just around the corner.

City Centre
Oranjezicht City Farm Market
Saturday 08:00–13:00 · Sunday 09:00–13:00

A community market with soul. Fresh produce from small-scale farmers, artisan bakers, and local food producers — all with Table Mountain as the backdrop. The Saturday market at the Waterfront and the Sunday market at De Waal Park are both excellent.

Website →
Dirk's Tip

The Sunday De Waal Park edition has a calmer, more local feel. Bring a blanket, grab a fresh juice, and sit on the grass with Table Mountain right above you. One of Cape Town's genuinely great free mornings.

Greenpoint · Weekend
Green Point Market
Weekends · 09:00–16:00

Craft, antiques, and African curios spread across the Green Point Urban Park. The largest outdoor market in Cape Town — great for unique gifts, handmade jewellery, and the occasional rare find. Relaxed atmosphere, easy to spend a few hours here.

Find on Maps →
Dirk's Tip

Combine with a run or walk through Green Point Urban Park and Sea Point Promenade. The market is best in the morning before it gets crowded. Good spot to find genuine African crafts at fair prices — just take your time to browse.

Stellenbosch · Day Trip
Stellenbosch Saturday Market
Every Saturday · 09:00–14:00

Combine a morning market with a wine tour. The Stellenbosch market sits in the heart of the historic university town, surrounded by Cape Dutch architecture and wine estates. Fresh food, local crafts, and easy access to the Winelands.

Find on Maps →
Dirk's Tip

Make a full Saturday of it — market in the morning, then head to Spier or Tokara for lunch and wine tasting. It's only 45 minutes from Cape Town and one of the best day-trip combinations I know.

Kalk Bay · Local favourite
Kalk Bay Harbour & Antiques
Daily · Best on weekends

Not a formal market — more a way of life. The strip of antique shops, galleries, and curio dealers along Main Road in Kalk Bay is one of the most rewarding browsing experiences in Cape Town. End with fish & chips at Kalky's on the harbour.

Find on Maps →
Dirk's Tip

Go on a weekday if you can — Kalk Bay on weekends gets very busy. Take the train from Cape Town station for a proper local experience. Walk the harbour, watch the seals, then browse the antique shops. Finish at Kalky's for the best fish & chips in the city.

V&A Waterfront
V&A Food Market
Daily · 10:00–20:00

A permanent indoor food market inside the Waterfront. Over 40 food stalls covering everything from sushi to South African braai. Great for a quick lunch with harbour views. More polished than the Neighbourgoods crowd, but excellent quality.

Website →
Dirk's Tip

The best option when you're already at the Waterfront and need a proper lunch without committing to a sit-down restaurant. The Cape Malay curry and the wood-fired pizza stalls are my go-to. Open daily — reliable and always busy for good reason.
