The honest beach guide

The water is always cold. The views are always extraordinary. Here's where to go.

Cape Town's beaches are some of the most beautiful in the world — and some of the most misunderstood. The Atlantic side is cold, dramatic, and gorgeous. The False Bay side is warmer and wilder. The SE wind (Cape Doctor) can ruin a beach day instantly — always check the forecast before you go. With that said: these six beaches are extraordinary.

Dirk's Take
"The most secret beach in Cape Town. Scramble down the rocks from Bakoven and you have your own private cove. Crystal clear tidal pool. Almost nobody knows it's there."

Hidden between Camps Bay and Clifton, Beta Beach is Cape Town's best-kept secret. Park on Victoria Road in Bakoven, scramble down the rocks, and discover a sheltered tidal pool and cove that feels completely private. This is what Clifton felt like 30 years ago.

Insider Tips
→ Park on Victoria Road, Bakoven — look for the path down the rocks
→ Bring beach shoes — the scramble down is rocky
→ Morning is best — goes into shade after 3pm
→ Protected from the SE wind — when Camps Bay is a sandstorm, this is perfect

The honest truth

The water is always cold.
Here's how cold.

The Benguela Current brings icy Antarctic water up the Atlantic coast year-round. Even in the height of summer (January–February), Atlantic-side water temperatures rarely exceed 16°C. False Bay (Muizenberg, Fish Hoek) is warmer — sometimes reaching 22°C in summer. Plan accordingly.

⚠️
Beach Safety — Read This
Cape Town's Atlantic beaches have no lifeguards outside peak season. The shore break at Llandudno and Noordhoek is powerful — not suitable for weak swimmers. The SE wind (Cape Doctor) creates dangerous conditions instantly — if the flags are up, don't swim. Always check Windguru or Windy before heading to the beach. Muizenberg and Fish Hoek (False Bay) are the safest beaches for families and weak swimmers.
