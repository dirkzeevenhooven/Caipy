You're packing for Cape Town Summer

November to April is Cape Town's summer — long days, strong sun, warm evenings, and the famous Cape Doctor wind. Think light layers, serious sun protection, and at least one smart-casual outfit for Camps Bay evenings.

The Essentials

📄 Documents
💳 Money & Cards
🔌 Adaptor & Power
Dirk's Tip

South Africa still experiences occasional load shedding (scheduled power cuts). A charged power bank is your best friend on those days. Also — photograph all your documents on your phone and email them to yourself before you leave. If your bag is stolen, you'll be glad you did.

Clothing

Cape Town's dress code is relaxed but not sloppy. Smart casual gets you into almost every restaurant. Pack light — laundry is easy and cheap.

☀️ Summer (Nov–Apr)
🌧️ Winter (May–Oct)
Dirk's Tip

The Cape Doctor — Cape Town's famous south-easterly wind — can make a 28°C summer day feel genuinely cold on Table Mountain or the Boulders Beach boardwalk. Always pack a windbreaker even in peak summer. The wind drops in the evenings, so don't let it stop you from going up the mountain — just bring the layer.

Dirk's Tip

Cape Town tap water is some of the best in Africa — clean, fresh mountain water. Bring a reusable bottle and refill it everywhere. You'll save money and reduce plastic. Most restaurants and cafés will fill it for free if you ask.

Health & Pharmacy

Cape Town has excellent pharmacies (Dis-Chem, Clicks) where you can buy almost anything on arrival. These are the things worth bringing from home.

Tech & Connectivity

Stay connected, charged, and ready to capture every moment.

📱 Tech Essentials
🚗 Driving in SA
Dirk's Tip

Buy a local Vodacom prepaid SIM at the airport the moment you land — it's R50 and gives you 1GB of data. Top it up via the Vodacom app or any supermarket. Don't rely on your home roaming plan — the costs are brutal and the local data is fast and cheap. Vodacom has the best coverage on the Cape Peninsula.

Leave Behind

Things that take up space unnecessarily — Cape Town has everything, and most of this you can buy on arrival for less.

Don't bother packing these

Save the space and buy locally if you need them — or just don't need them at all.

  • Heavy guidebooks — this guide has everything
  • Formal wear — smart casual is the maximum dress code
  • Multiple pairs of jeans — takes too much space, dries slowly
  • Hair dryer — hotels provide them, or buy for R150 at Clicks
  • Malaria tablets — not needed for Cape Town
  • Mosquito net — not needed for the Western Cape
  • Huge first aid kit — Dis-Chem pharmacies are everywhere
  • Bottled water from home — Cape tap water is excellent
  • Lots of cash — cards accepted almost everywhere
  • Expensive jewellery — leave it at home, not worth the risk
  • Too many shoes — 3 pairs maximum (flip flops, walkers, smart)
  • Beach umbrella — hire at most beaches for R50/day
Dirk's Tip

Woolworths Food in Cape Town stocks excellent quality toiletries, sunscreen, and travel essentials at very reasonable prices. Pick up what you need on day one rather than lugging it from home. Their sunscreen range is particularly good and significantly cheaper than airport prices.
