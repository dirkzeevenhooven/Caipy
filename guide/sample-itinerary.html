The Itinerary

Day One · Arrival
Settle In
Sea Point · V&A Waterfront · Bree Street
Morning
Land, transfer, unpack. Cape Town rewards the rested traveller — don't try to cram the first afternoon. Book a car through the airport desks or collect your pre-booked rental from Sixt or Hertz (both in the arrivals hall).
Afternoon
Walk the Sea Point Promenade — 3km of coastal path with Table Mountain behind you and the Atlantic ahead. Real Cape Town in plain sight. End at the Sea Point Pavilion tidal pool.
Evening
Sunset drinks at Camps Bay strip — Café Caprice or Koi Bar. Then Bree Street for dinner: Superette, Wren, or Bocca. Book ahead. Budget R250–400 per person.
Insider Tip
Book Bree Street dinner before you land — good tables go Thursday to Saturday within hours of opening.
Safety
Uber from airport to accommodation. Uber back from Bree Street at night. Never walk alone late in unfamiliar areas.
Bad Weather Plan
Skip the promenade — head straight to the V&A Waterfront. The Two Oceans Aquarium is excellent on a rainy first afternoon.
Day Two · Icons
Table Mountain
Cable Car · Bo-Kaap · Kloof Street
Morning
Book the Table Mountain cable car for 8:30am — early gives you the best light and fewest crowds. Check the cloud cover the night before. 360° views of both oceans, the Peninsula and Robben Island. Allow 2 hours on top.
Afternoon
Bo-Kaap — cobblestone streets, pastel houses, 300 years of Cape Malay history. Wander, photograph. Then drive up Signal Hill for a second panoramic view. Sunset from here is one of the best in Cape Town.
Evening
Dinner on Kloof Street. Try The Black Sheep (book ahead), La Mouette for something refined, or walk the strip and choose by feel — the vibe changes every 50 metres.
Insider Tip
Book the cable car online the night before — R380 return. If you miss the early slot, Signal Hill is a free sunset alternative.
Safety
Bo-Kaap is safe in the day — go before 10am for photos without crowds. Don't photograph locals without permission.
Bad Weather Plan
If the mountain is clouded out, do Robben Island instead — a deeply moving 3.5-hour experience in any weather.
Day Three · Peninsula
Cape Point
Chapman's Peak · Boulders Beach · Cape of Good Hope
Early Start
Leave by 7:30am. Drive Chapman's Peak — one of the most dramatic coastal roads on earth. Stop at every viewpoint. Allow 1 hour if you do it properly. Then Boulders Beach — 3,000 African penguins that walk up to you. R220 entry. Extraordinary.
Afternoon
Drive to the Cape of Good Hope — the dramatic southwestern tip of Africa. Flying Dutchman funicular saves the climb. Walk the trails, feel the wind. Stop at Simon's Town on the way back — lunch at Seaforth Restaurant, tables right on the water.
Evening
Sunset at Noordhoek Beach — 8km of wild, deserted sand with Chapman's Peak cliffs turning pink. Dinner at The Foodbarn at Noordhoek Farm Village. Book ahead. One of the best meals on the Peninsula.
Insider Tip
Baboons at Cape Point are wild and bold — lock your car, never feed them, keep windows closed. They will open a door if you leave it unlocked.
Safety
Full 10–11 hour day. You need a hire car. Roads are safe and well-maintained. Don't drive Chapman's Peak in heavy rain — it closes.
Bad Weather Plan
Do Boulders Beach only (penguins don't mind the rain) and swap Cape Point for Kalk Bay harbour, galleries and coffee.
Day Four · Winelands
Constantia & Kirstenbosch
Groot Constantia · Klein Constantia · Botanical Gardens
Morning
Drive to Constantia — the oldest wine-producing area in the Southern Hemisphere, 20 minutes from the city. Start at Groot Constantia (1685, South Africa's oldest estate), then Klein Constantia for their famous Vin de Constance dessert wine. Tastings from R100–200 per person.
Afternoon
Kirstenbosch National Botanical Garden — built into the eastern slopes of Table Mountain. Walk the treetop canopy walkway, explore Cape Fynbos, picnic on the lawn. Allow 2–3 hours. Entry R220. In summer, check for Sunday concerts — legendary.
Evening
If you want something exceptional: La Colombe in Constantia — one of Africa's best restaurants. Or return to Bree Street for somewhere you missed on Night 1.
Insider Tip
Designate a driver or use Uber between estates. A wine tour driver costs R600–900 for the day — genuinely worth it.
Safety
Constantia is one of Cape Town's safest suburbs. Kirstenbosch closes at 6pm in winter, 7pm in summer.
Bad Weather Plan
Wine estates are perfect in any weather. Skip Kirstenbosch, add a third estate — Buitenverwachting has an exceptional restaurant for a long rainy lunch.
Day Five · Culture
Waterfront & Zeitz
V&A Waterfront · Zeitz MOCAA · Send-off dinner
Morning
Walk the V&A Waterfront — the working harbour with Table Mountain as backdrop. One of the great urban views on earth. Then visit Zeitz Museum of Contemporary African Art — the world's largest museum of contemporary African art, inside a converted grain silo. Allow 2 hours. Extraordinary building alone.
Afternoon
Explore De Waterkant (Cape Town's design district) or browse Neighbourgoods Market (Saturday only at Old Biscuit Mill). If you haven't done it — Lion's Head hike takes 2 hours and gives arguably the best view in Cape Town.
Evening
Final dinner off the tourist trail. The Pot Luck Club in Woodstock (small plates, rooftop, book ahead), or Harbour House in Kalk Bay (seafood, right on the harbour). Ask your accommodation for their honest recommendation — always the best option on the last night.
Insider Tip
Zeitz MOCAA is closed on Tuesdays. Book tickets online. The Silo Hotel rooftop bar above it is an incredible sundowner even if you're not staying.
Safety
The Waterfront is safe at all hours. Woodstock is fine in the day — use Uber at night. Don't walk from Woodstock to the city centre after dark.
Bad Weather Plan
A full rainy day at Zeitz MOCAA plus lunch and shopping at the V&A Waterfront is genuinely enjoyable.
