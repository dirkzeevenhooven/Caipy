Planet Uncharted · Safety & Preparation

The Honest
Safety Guide

Not the sanitised tourist version. The real talk — from someone who has lived in Cape Town for years and wants you to have an extraordinary, safe trip.

🗣️
Dirk's honest take
"Cape Town is safe for tourists who are switched on. It is not safe for tourists who are careless. The difference between a great trip and a terrible one is almost always awareness — not luck."

Cape Town has real safety challenges, particularly around vehicle crime and petty theft. But the tourist areas — Sea Point, Camps Bay, the Waterfront, the Winelands, the Garden Route — are genuinely safe when you follow basic rules. Millions of tourists visit every year without incident. This guide gives you everything you need to be one of them.

✅ Safe for tourists
Sea Point & Green Point — Best base, safe at night, promenade, restaurants
Camps Bay & Clifton — Tourist area, safe day and night, busy strip
V&A Waterfront — Fully managed, security everywhere, very safe
City Bowl (CBD daytime) — Safe during business hours, avoid after dark
Stellenbosch & Franschhoek — Wine towns, very safe, tourist-friendly
Simon's Town & Kalk Bay — Coastal villages, safe and relaxed
Garden Route — Knysna, George, Plettenberg Bay — very safe
Constantia & Southern Suburbs — Residential, safe, wine estates
⚠️ Avoid as a tourist
Cape Flats — Township areas: Khayelitsha, Mitchells Plain — not for tourists without guided tours
CBD after dark — Long Street area late at night without a group
Salt River & Woodstock (late night) — Fine during the day, avoid after 10pm alone
Any isolated area after dark — Mountain paths, beaches, parking areas alone at night
Unmarked taxis — Never, under any circumstances. Use Uber exclusively.
The non-negotiable rules
🚗
Never leave anything in your car
🔴 Critical
Not on the seat. Not in the footwell. Not "hidden" under a jacket. Nothing visible in a parked car — ever. Cape Town has a serious smash-and-grab problem. If it's in your car, put it in the boot before you park — not when you arrive at your destination.
📱
Bring your old phone
🔴 Critical
Leave your new iPhone in the hotel safe. Bring an older or cheaper smartphone for daily use. Never use your phone while walking — phone snatching is the most common crime against tourists in Cape Town. Keep it in your pocket, not your hand.
🚕
Uber only — no exceptions
🔴 Critical
Use Uber exclusively for all transport. Never get into an unmarked taxi or a vehicle that approaches you offering a ride. Uber is cheap, safe, and works perfectly everywhere in Cape Town. It's R80–150 for most city trips.
🏧
ATM safety
🟡 Important
Use ATMs inside banks or shopping centres — never freestanding street ATMs. Cover your PIN always. If anyone approaches you while at an ATM, cancel and leave immediately. Withdraw larger amounts less frequently rather than small amounts often.
👜
Bag and valuables awareness
🟡 Important
Wear bags across your body, not on one shoulder. Don't flash expensive cameras, watches, or jewellery in public spaces. At restaurants, loop your bag strap around your chair leg. Leave your passport in the hotel safe — carry a photo of it instead.
🌙
After dark awareness
🟡 Important
Tourist areas like Sea Point, Camps Bay, and the Waterfront are safe at night. The CBD and areas like De Waterkant are fine in groups. Avoid walking alone late at night in any area — Uber is always the right call after 10pm.
🏖️
Beach and outdoor safety
🟢 Good to know
Don't leave valuables unattended on the beach. Most beaches in tourist areas are safe during the day. Check rip currents before swimming — Atlantic beaches have powerful shore breaks. Lifeguards are only present in peak season at main beaches.
🏔️
Mountain and hiking safety
🟢 Good to know
Never hike alone on Table Mountain or Lion's Head. Tell someone your route and expected return time. Carry water, sunscreen, and a wind layer. Weather changes fast — if the tablecloth forms, descend immediately. Mountain Rescue: 021 937 0300.
🚘
Car guard etiquette
🟢 Good to know
Informal car guards are everywhere in Cape Town. They are not official but they do watch your car. Give R10–20 when you return — it's expected and helps prevent problems. If there's no guard, use a secure parking area when possible.
✅ Use these

Safe transport options

📱
Uber — The gold standard. Safe, metered, driver tracked. Use for everything.
🚌
MyCiTi Bus — Good for Sea Point to Waterfront route. Safe and air-conditioned.
🚗
Rental car — Essential for day trips. Book in advance. Lock always.
🏨
Hotel transfers — Always an option for airport arrival. Worth it for peace of mind.
⚠️ Avoid these

Transport to stay away from

🚕
Unmarked taxis — Never. Not negotiable. Stories do not end well.
🚐
Minibus taxis (kombis) — Local transport, not for tourists. Unsafe and confusing.
🚶
Walking at night alone — Even in safe areas, take Uber after 10pm.
🚗
Accepting rides from strangers — Never, regardless of how convincing the story.

What to bring vs what to leave at the hotel
✅ Bring daily
✓ Photo of your passport (not the original)
✓ Older or secondary smartphone
✓ Small amount of cash (R200–400 max)
✓ One credit card (not all your cards)
✓ Travel insurance details and emergency number
✓ Sunscreen, water, windproof layer for outdoors
❌ Leave in hotel safe
✗ Your actual passport
✗ Your newest, most expensive phone
✗ Expensive jewellery and watches
✗ Multiple credit cards
✗ Large amounts of cash
✗ Laptop (unless you need it)
⚠️ Common tourist scams — know these
The scams Dirk sees tourists fall for every season
The "Friendly Local" scam
Someone approaches, is extremely friendly, offers to show you around. Eventually asks for money, a "loan", or leads you somewhere unsafe. Trust your instincts — politely decline and walk away.
The ATM "helper"
Someone offers to "help" you at an ATM, often claiming the machine is broken or you need a code. They are watching your PIN or card-swapping. Use ATMs inside banks only, alone.
The fake "official" parking
Someone in a vest claims to be official parking and demands upfront payment. Real car guards are informal — pay R10–20 when you return, not before. Never pay upfront.
The bottom line

Cape Town is extraordinary.
Go. Just go smart.

Millions of tourists visit Cape Town every year and have the trip of their lives without any incident. The city is spectacular, the people are warm, and the experiences are world-class. Follow the rules in this guide, stay switched on, and you will have an extraordinary time. Don't let fear stop you from going — let preparation make you confident.
