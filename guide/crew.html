The 3-Day Layover Plan

Three days, paced for crew.

Land soft, peak in the middle, wind down before duty. No day overloads the next.

Day 1 · Arrive & reset

Recover & ease in

  • Slow morning, hydrate, short Sea Point promenade walk
  • Easy, healthy lunch near your hotel
  • Low-effort sunset — Sea Point or Camps Bay
  • Early, light dinner; protect your sleep
Day 2 · Your big day

Choose your adventure

  • Active: Lion’s Head or Pipe Track in the morning
  • Beach: Camps Bay or Clifton, lunch on the strip
  • Wine: Constantia half-day, vineyard lunch
  • City: V&A, Bo-Kaap, Company’s Garden
Day 3 · Pre-duty

Light & low-risk

  • Great coffee and a relaxed breakfast
  • Easy flat walk — promenade or V&A
  • No alcohol, no sun-overload, no big effort
  • Pack early; rest before report time

Active Cape Town

Move, with a view.

Short, scenic and crew-friendly — earn the sunset without wrecking tomorrow.

Lion’s Head
The iconic Cape Town climb — roughly 1.5–2 hrs return. Go early or for sunset; take water and grippy shoes.
Signal Hill
Minimal effort, maximum payoff. Drive or short walk up for the city’s best easy sunset.
Pipe Track
A gentle, mostly-flat contour trail under the Twelve Apostles — great low-impact option with sea views.
Promenade walks
The Sea Point promenade is flat, safe by day and perfect for a recovery walk or run.
Beaches
Camps Bay, Clifton and Llandudno for Atlantic beauty; the water is cold but the views are worth it.
Wind, weather & safety: Cape Town’s “Cape Doctor” south-easter can be fierce — check the wind before any hike, start early, carry water, and turn back in bad conditions. Hike in pairs and stick to popular, well-used trails.
Wine & Group Options

Vineyards & group days.

The easiest way for a crew to spend a day well — someone else drives, you enjoy.

Constantia
Half-day, closest to the city. Historic estates, vineyard lunches, back before sunset — ideal on a 3-day layover.
Stellenbosch / Franschhoek
Full-day winelands with world-class estates. Best with a driver so everyone can taste freely.
Private driver / bus
A chauffeured car or small bus is the crew favourite — no parking, no nominated driver, door to door.
Small group planning
Book the driver a day ahead, agree on 2–3 estates, lock a lunch reservation, and keep a soft finish time before duty.
Crew tip: Wine days are best mid-layover, never the day before duty. Share the driver cost across the group and you’ll often beat the price of individual Ubers.
Low-Energy Day

Do almost nothing, beautifully.

For the day after a long-haul, when recovery is the whole plan.

Easy breakfast
Slow start, good coffee and a clean plate near your hotel — no rush, no agenda.
Sea Point promenade
A flat, gentle walk by the ocean. Fresh air and movement without effort or risk.
Spa / recovery
A massage or hotel spa session to reset body clock and tired legs.
Sunset without effort
Sea Point or Signal Hill — drive up, watch the sky, be home in minutes.
Good food, close by
Stay near common crew areas (Sea Point, V&A, City Bowl) so dinner is a short, safe hop.

Safety & Getting Around

Move smart, land smooth.

Common-sense crew guidance — Cape Town rewards a little planning.

Uber vs rental
Uber/Bolt are cheap, reliable and stress-free in the city — usually the best crew choice. A rental only pays off for full-day coastal or winelands trips.
Group transport
For 3+ people, a private driver or small bus is often cheaper and far easier than multiple cars or rides.
Late-night returns
Use Uber/Bolt door-to-door at night, share your trip with a colleague, and avoid walking alone after dark.
Before duty
Don’t overpack the day before report time — no big hikes, heavy sun or late nights. Hydrate and protect your rest.
Common sense
Keep valuables low-key, stick to busy areas, watch your belongings on the beach, and trust your instincts.
