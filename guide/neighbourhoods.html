Dirk's Field Note · May 2026
"Most visitors pick their hotel and then discover the neighbourhood. It should be the other way around."

I've watched people book a beautiful hotel in Camps Bay and spend half their holiday in an Uber because nothing is walkable. I've seen families land in the Waterfront and miss the real Cape Town entirely. And I've watched solo travellers plant themselves in Sea Point and never want to leave.

The neighbourhood is the decision. Get it right and everything else flows — the morning coffee spot you discover on day two, the restaurant you walk past on the way home, the promenade run you do every day. Get it wrong and you spend your trip in transit.

My honest take: Sea Point for first-timers, always. Then branch out from there.

Dirk Zeevenhooven
Founder, Planet Curated · 15 years in Cape Town

Sea Point

Walkable Local vibe Promenade Cafés Safe

The most walkable neighbourhood in Cape Town. A 5km promenade along the Atlantic, lined with restaurants, coffee shops, and the best people-watching in the city. Diverse, energetic, and unpretentious — this is where locals actually live. The safest and most convenient base for first-time visitors.

Best forCouples, solo travellers, anyone who wants convenience
Eat hereKleinsky's, La Mouette, Sea Point Rotisserie
Don't missThe Sea Point Pavilion — free ocean pools, open air
Walk toGreen Point, V&A Waterfront (20 min), Camps Bay (25 min)

Camps Bay

Beach clubs Sundowners Scenic Expensive Romantic

Glamorous, sunburnt, and beautiful. The beach strip backed by the Twelve Apostles mountain range is one of the most photographed spots in Africa. Expensive, yes — but worth it for sundowners at least once. Visit mid-week for a calmer experience; weekends get packed.

Best forRomantic trips, luxury seekers, beach lovers
Eat hereThe Bay Hotel terrace, Codfather (best sushi), Chinchilla
Don't missSunset from the beach — the mountain turns pink
WarningBaboons come down from the mountain — secure your picnic

De Waterkant & Green Point

Boutique hotels Restaurants Nightlife LGBTQ+ friendly

Boutique hotels, Cape Dutch architecture, and some of the best restaurants in the city. Walking distance to the V&A Waterfront and the Cape Town Stadium. A great base if you want central without touristy. De Waterkant has a vibrant LGBTQ+ scene and strong nightlife.

Best forNightlife seekers, foodies, cultural explorers
Eat hereThe Chefs Warehouse & Canteen, Loading Bay, Burrata
Don't missCape Quarter shopping arcade — independent boutiques
Walk toV&A Waterfront (10 min), Sea Point (15 min)

Bo-Kaap

Cultural Photography History Cape Malay Go early

The iconic multicoloured neighbourhood on the slopes of Signal Hill. Home to the Cape Malay community for 200+ years. Visit for the architecture, the history, and the best samosas you'll find anywhere. Go before 8am — by 10am the tour buses arrive and the magic fades.

Best forPhotography, cultural immersion, history lovers
Eat hereBiesmiellah (Cape Malay classics since 1969), Bo Kaap Kombuis
Don't missBo-Kaap Museum — R30 entry, genuinely fascinating
Best timeBefore 8am — empty streets, golden morning light

Bree Street & Gardens

Fine dining Wine bars Independent shops Foodie central

The heartbeat of Cape Town's food and creative scene. Bree Street is lined with independent restaurants, wine bars, and concept stores. This is where chefs open their passion projects — every dinner feels like an event. Book 2–3 weeks ahead for weekends.

Best forFoodies, wine lovers, creative crowd
Eat hereFyn, The Shortmarket Club, Chefs Warehouse Beau Constantia
Don't missThe Company's Garden — oldest garden in South Africa
TipBook restaurants 2–3 weeks ahead for weekends

Constantia & Newlands

Wine estates Hiking Family-friendly Lush Peaceful

Cape Town's wine valley hidden in plain sight. Groot Constantia — the oldest wine estate in South Africa — sits here alongside Klein Constantia, Buitenverwachting and Steenberg. Lush, green, and cooler than the coast. Newlands has Kirstenbosch — arguably the world's most beautiful botanical garden.

Best forFamilies, wine lovers, nature seekers
Don't missKirstenbosch — Sunday evening concerts in summer are magical
Eat hereConstantia Uitsig, La Colombe, Buitenverwachting restaurant
Getting here20 min from city centre — Uber works fine

Waterfront

Shopping Ferries Harbour views Touristy Seals

Tourist central — but good tourist central. The working harbour, seals on the jetty, Robben Island ferries, and 400+ shops and restaurants. Don't skip it — just don't eat at the obvious places. The Watershed market inside has local designers, great food stalls, and far better prices.

Best forFamilies, shopping, Robben Island trips
Don't missRobben Island ferry — book online, sells out fast
Eat hereHarbour House, Quay Four (upstairs), The V&A Food Market
TipThe seals hang around Quay 5 — free entertainment

Kalk Bay & Simon's Town

Penguins Antiques Local food Fishing village Day trip

The most characterful stretch of the peninsula. Kalk Bay has a working harbour, independent galleries, and the best fish & chips in Cape Town (Kalky's — cash only, eat on the harbour wall). Simon's Town is home to the African penguin colony at Boulders Beach — unmissable.

Best forDay trips, antique hunters, penguin fans
Don't missBoulders Beach penguins — buy tickets in advance online
Eat hereKalky's (fish & chips), Harbour House Kalk Bay, Empire Café
Getting here45 min from Sea Point, or scenic train from Cape Town station

Woodstock & Observatory

Street art Markets Creative scene Foodie Bohemian

Cape Town's creative heartland. Woodstock is home to the Neighbourgoods Market (the best in the city) and a sprawling street art scene. Observatory is the student and bohemian neighbourhood — cheap eats, live music, vinyl shops, and a completely different energy from the tourist belt.

Best forCreatives, foodies, market lovers, younger travellers
Don't missNeighbourgoods Market at the Old Biscuit Mill — Saturdays only
Eat hereThe Test Kitchen (Woodstock), Pot Luck Club, Superette
TipStick to Albert Road and Lower Main — avoid isolated streets at night
