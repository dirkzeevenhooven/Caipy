Best Time to Visit
When to go — and when to avoid
Cape Town has a Mediterranean climate — hot, dry summers and mild, wet winters. The best time to visit is October to April, with December and January being peak season. Avoid January and February if you can — it's extremely crowded and expensive. The shoulder months of October–November and March–April are often the best combination of good weather and manageable crowds.
Summer
Dec · Jan · Feb
★★★★★
Hot and sunny. Peak crowds and prices. Book everything 3+ months ahead. SE wind can be strong.
Shoulder ✨
Oct · Nov · Mar · Apr
★★★★★
Dirk's recommendation. Perfect weather, fewer crowds, reasonable prices. Best wildflowers in Sep–Oct.
Winter
Jun · Jul · Aug
★★★★★
Cool and wet. Great for wine country, whale watching starts Aug. Half the price of peak season.
Whale Season 🐋
Aug · Sep · Oct · Nov
★★★★★
Southern Right Whales in Walker Bay. Best wildlife experience in Cape Town. Combine with Hermanus.

💡 Dirk's money tips: Carry R500–800 cash daily for markets, car guards, and small purchases. Use credit card for restaurants and shops. Withdraw larger amounts less frequently. FNB and Standard Bank ATMs are most reliable. Dynamic currency conversion at ATMs — always choose to pay in ZAR.

Electricity & Adapters
South Africa uses Type M plugs
Voltage
230V / 50Hz
Same as Europe. US devices (110V) need a voltage converter, not just an adapter.
Plug Type
Type M (3 round pins)
Buy a universal travel adapter before you leave. Available at CPT airport but overpriced.
Load Shedding
Eskom schedules
Power outages are less frequent now but still possible. Download the EskomSePush app to check your area's schedule.

"That wine is lekker" — the most useful word in SA
BraaiBBQ / Barbecue
South Africa's sacred ritual. Never call it a BBQ.
EishWow / Oh no / Really?
Universal expression of surprise or mild dismay
HowzitHow are you / Hi
