The Plan

Day-by-day itinerary

Day
1
The City Icons
Table Mountain · Bo-Kaap · Bree Street
7:30 am
Table Mountain
⚡ Energy: Moderate 👥 Crowds: Moderate
City Bowl
Beat the cable car queue. Arrive at the lower station by 8am. Check cloud cover the night before on yr.no. If cloudy: Lion's Head instead — better views when Table Mountain has its tablecloth.
→ See guide
11:00 am
Bo-Kaap
⚡ Energy: Easy 👥 Crowds: Moderate
Bo-Kaap
Walk from the cable car down to the city. Bo-Kaap is 20 minutes by Uber. Come for the pastel houses and the history — be there before noon before the tour groups arrive.
2:00 pm
V&A Waterfront
⚡ Energy: Easy 👥 Crowds: Busy
Waterfront
Not for the restaurants (overpriced) but for the seals, the views, and the Zeitz MOCAA if you're into contemporary African art.
7:00 pm
Bree Street Dinner
⚡ Energy: Easy 👥 Crowds: Moderate
Bree Street
Pick any restaurant on the strip. Farro, Haiku, or Bocca all excellent. Book ahead.
→ See guide
🌬️ If conditions change
Windy or rainy day alternative
Skip Table Mountain (not safe). Do Zeitz MOCAA + Bree Street lunch + Constantia wine estate instead. This is actually a brilliant rainy day — arguably more interesting than the original plan.
Day
2
The Peninsula
Chapman's Peak · Cape Point · Penguins · Kalk Bay
7:30 am
Chapman's Peak Drive
⚡ Energy: Easy 👥 Crowds: Quiet
Hout Bay
Leave by 7:30am. Drive the full pass — stop at every viewpoint. R52 toll, worth every cent. Morning light on the Atlantic is extraordinary.
9:30 am
Cape Point + Cape of Good Hope
⚡ Energy: Active 👥 Crowds: Moderate
Cape Peninsula
Walk to the old lighthouse. Two oceans meet here. Allow 2 hours. Combine with the funicular up to the lighthouse.
→ See guide
1:30 pm
Boulders Beach Penguins
⚡ Energy: Easy 👥 Crowds: Moderate
Simonstown
African penguins on a pristine beach. R220 entry. Genuinely magical — the penguins come right up to you. Allow 90 minutes.
→ See guide
4:00 pm
Kalk Bay Harbour
⚡ Energy: Easy 👥 Crowds: Quiet
Kalk Bay
Fish & chips from the boats. Antique shops on the main street. One of the most authentic parts of Cape Town.
🔄 If conditions change
Alternative Peninsula routes
If Chapman's Peak is closed (check SA National Parks): drive via Hout Bay — still beautiful. If Boulders is too crowded (peak season Dec/Jan): go to Simonstown and walk the boardwalk instead.
Day
3
Wine & Culture
Markets · Winelands · Signal Hill · Farewell Dinner
9:00 am
Neighbourgoods Market (Sat only)
⚡ Energy: Easy 👥 Crowds: Busy
Woodstock
The Old Biscuit Mill in Woodstock. Cape Town's best market. Food, coffee, design. Get there early. If not Saturday: Oranjezicht City Farm Market at Granger Bay instead.
11:00 am
Durbanville Hills / Diemersdal
⚡ Energy: Easy 👥 Crowds: Quiet
Durbanville
The insider wine estate. 20 minutes from the city. Smaller, no crowds, you can meet the winemaker. Order the Sauvignon Blanc.
→ See guide
5:30 pm
Signal Hill Sundowner
⚡ Energy: Easy 👥 Crowds: Quiet
Signal Hill
Drive up Signal Hill 30 minutes before sunset. Park at the lookout. Bring a bottle of the wine you bought this morning. Watch the city go golden. Free.
7:30 pm
Farewell Dinner
⚡ Energy: Easy 👥 Crowds: Moderate
City Centre
The Test Kitchen (book 6 weeks ahead) for a true occasion. Or Pot Luck Club for excellent food with city views. Or back to Bree Street if you found something you loved.
→ See guide
🌧️ If it rains
Rainy day wine alternative
Constantia wine estates are beautiful in rain. Groot Constantia + Buitenverwachting in the same afternoon. Take the Peninsula road via Hout Bay.
The Backup Plans

When things change — and they will.

Cape Town weather is theatrical. Here's how to adapt without losing a day.
☀️
Perfect Day

Drop everything and go to Beta Beach (Bakoven) or Llandudno. Bring lunch, water, and sunscreen. These are Cape Town's most beautiful beaches — no crowds, no vendors, just ocean. Come back to the itinerary tomorrow.

🌬️
Strong SE Wind (Cape Doctor)

Skip beaches and the mountain. Perfect day for: Constantia wine estates (sheltered valley), Zeitz MOCAA + gallery circuit, Neighbourgoods Market if Saturday, or drive to Stellenbosch. The Cape Doctor blows cold and hard — lean into the inland day.

🌧️
Rain

Cape Town in rain is still great. Best options: Zeitz MOCAA, Norval Foundation (sculpture garden + café), wine estates in Constantia valley, Kalk Bay antique shops + coffee. Rain in Constantia is actually beautiful — bring a jacket and embrace it.

👥
Peak Season (Dec–Jan)

Move Table Mountain to a weekday at 7am. Skip Clifton 4th — go to Beta Beach or Llandudno. Avoid Neighbourgoods Market — go to Oranjezicht City Farm Market instead. Book all restaurants minimum 2 days ahead. The city is full but the peninsula still has quiet corners.
