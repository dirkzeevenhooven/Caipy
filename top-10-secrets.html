🌅 Viewpoints

Signal Hill at Sunset — Better Than Camps Bay

Everyone crowds Camps Bay. The locals drive up Signal Hill with a bottle of wine.

Signal Hill sits above Sea Point and gives you a panoramic 270° view of the Atlantic Ocean, Robben Island, and the entire city below. Drive up just before 6pm and watch the sun sink into the ocean with Table Mountain lit gold behind you. Bring a blanket, a bottle of local Sauvignon Blanc (R60 from Pick n Pay), and cheese from Giovanni's Deli. Free, magical, and almost entirely yours while the tour groups queue for Camps Bay sundowners.

◆ Insider Pick

Best Nov–Mar on a clear evening. Arrive 45 min before sunset. Wrap up — the South-Easter can be brisk even in summer.

🚂 Getting Around

The R30 Coastal Train: Your Private Sightseeing Express

Tourists pay hundreds for coastal drives. Locals pay R30 for a more beautiful one.

The Metrorail Simon's Town line is one of the most scenic train journeys in Africa. For 40 minutes it hugs the False Bay coastline — mountains on one side, whales in season on the other. Take it from Cape Town Station to Simon's Town on a weekday morning in First Class only (R30–60 return). The views between Kalk Bay and Fish Hoek are genuinely extraordinary: crashing waves right next to the tracks.

◆ Insider Rule

First Class only — always. Go mid-morning on a weekday. Take the 9:30am from Cape Town Station.

🏊 Free Experiences

Sea Point Tidal Pools — Cape Town's Open Secret

A free ocean pool used by thousands of locals every single day.

Running along the Sea Point Promenade is a full-length sea pool — completely free. At 7am you'll find serious lap swimmers, elderly Capetonians, toddlers splashing, and everything in between. The water is Atlantic cold (14–16°C) but clean and bracing. Walk the promenade from Sea Point to Green Point at sunrise for one of the great free Cape Town experiences.

◆ Go At

Early morning or late afternoon on weekdays for the local experience. Weekends get busy but the energy is brilliant. No sharks — it's a tidal pool.

🎵 Events

Kirstenbosch Summer Concerts: Picnic Under Table Mountain

Every Sunday from December to March. Wine, music, and Table Mountain as your backdrop.

The Kirstenbosch Summer Sunset Concerts run every Sunday from early December to late March at 5:30pm. World-class botanical gardens, live performers, and the rear slopes of Table Mountain towering directly above you. Bring a picnic basket, a blanket, local wine (buy before you enter — R60–80/bottle), and friends. Gates open at 4pm. The light turns golden at 6pm, the mountain turns orange at 7pm.

◆ Local Tip

Arrive at 4pm. Set up facing the mountain, not the stage — you want that backdrop. Buy wine at Checkers en route. Uber back — parking is chaos.

🦭 Wildlife

Kayak to Seal Island, Hout Bay

Most tourists see seals from a boat deck. You can kayak among 75,000 of them.

Hout Bay harbour sits 2km from Duiker Island — home to one of the largest Cape fur seal colonies in Africa. While tourist boats circle at a distance, sea kayak tours take you right into the colony. The seals are curious, enormous, and will swim alongside your kayak. Cost: around R650–800 per person including equipment. One of Cape Town's most genuinely unique wildlife experiences.

◆ Book Via

Lebo's Sea Kayaking or Real Cape Adventures are the main operators. Morning slots are limited — book 2–3 days ahead in season.

☕ Coffee

Truth Coffee: The World's Best Coffee Shop

Voted the world's best coffee shop by multiple publications. Hidden in plain sight in the CBD.

Truth Coffee on Buitenkant Street is housed in a dramatic steampunk-themed converted Victorian building: exposed copper pipes, vintage espresso machines, taxidermy, and a barista team that takes single-origin coffee with complete seriousness. Named world's best coffee shop by the Telegraph and others. Go on a weekday before 9am to get a seat. A flat white runs around R50–55.

◆ Also Worth Knowing

Rosetta Roastery on Bree Street is Truth's rival for the more minimalist, specialty-purist experience. Both are exceptional.

🌺 Hidden Gems

Rhodes Memorial: The View No One Tells You About

Hidden above UCT, behind granite lions — one of the best city panoramas in Cape Town. Almost always deserted.

Rhodes Memorial sits on the slopes of Devil's Peak, just above the University of Cape Town. A neoclassical monument flanked by bronze lions guards a broad granite staircase with spectacular views over the Cape Flats toward the Winelands. The Memorial Tea House serves excellent coffee, scones, and light lunches with outdoor tables. Free, almost never crowded, and close to the city.

◆ Heads Up

Baboons are real and bold. Don't leave windows open in your car. Tea House open 9am–5pm daily. Coffee: R40. Worth every cent.

🛒 Food & Drink

Giovanni's Deli: Build the Perfect Picnic

Cape Town's legendary deli. Every local knows it. Almost no tourist guide mentions it.

Giovanni's Deli & Café on Main Road in Green Point is a Cape Town institution. Floor-to-ceiling shelves of imported Italian goods, an extraordinary cheese counter, fresh-baked bread, charcuterie, olives, and the best sandwiches in the city. Build a picnic basket here before Clifton, Lion's Head, or Signal Hill. Good sourdough, aged cheddar, Cape snoek paté — under R250 for two people.

◆ The Order

Snoek paté on sourdough + aged Gruyère + mixed olives + Peroni. Ask for the cheese of the day — always exceptional.

🌸 Day Trips

West Coast Wildflowers: August's Hidden Show

Every August–September, the West Coast turns orange. 90 minutes north of Cape Town.

The Namaqualand daisy bloom is one of South Africa's greatest natural spectacles — and you can see it from Cape Town. West Coast National Park (120km north) transforms every August–September into a carpet of orange, purple, and white wildflowers stretching to the horizon. Combine with a stop at Paternoster village for the best seafood on the West Coast. Entry: R238/adult.

◆ Timing Matters

Aug 15 – Sep 15 is typical, but varies with rainfall. Check wildflowers.co.za for live conditions before you go.

🍷 Day Trips

Babylonstoren: The Most Beautiful Farm Estate in Africa

One hour from Cape Town. A 200-year-old wine estate with a garden that will reset your idea of what a farm can be.

Babylonstoren near Paarl is the most visually stunning farm estate in South Africa. A 200-year-old Cape Dutch manor house at the centre of a geometric kitchen garden with 300+ edible plants. Two extraordinary restaurants: Babel (farm-to-table) and Boeuf & Boeuf (open fire). Spend a full day: garden tour (R185), lunch at Babel, wine tasting, drive home in golden hour. Book ahead — it fills up.

◆ Must-Try Wine

Babylonstoren Chenin Blanc 2023 — around R180 at the estate. Their Mourvèdre rosé is also exceptional. Ask if they're still pouring it.
